This Trophy Palm Jumeirah Penthouse Brings a Fresh, Modern Sensibility to High-Rise Living in Dubai

Designer Charu Gandhi has transformed a raw, shell-and-core property into a warm and inviting family home in the sky
By Noni Ware
June 20, 2025

Inspired by the land and the sea, the calming interior of this duplex penthouse on Palm Jumeirah in Dubai belies its staggering scale.

There are many skills that a talented interior designer must utilise when they embark on a new project. The ability to bring a vision to reality, to liaise with clients and contractors and, like a magician, to conjure a home from technical drawings into a warm and welcoming home. When the home is a 23,000 sq ft duplex penthouse apartment in One Palm Jumeirah Residences, which was initially just a raw shell, there was more than a little magic needed. The designer in question, Charu Gandhi from Elicyon, knew that the secret to making this lofty space work for her clients was going to require a lot of careful planning and collaboration. “The sheer scale of the penthouse was both exhilarating and complex to design,” says Gandhi. “Our approach was to create a sequence of purposeful zones that function for the family at all times.”

From the outset, the brief for this Palm Jumeirah penthouse was to create a home that felt luxurious but was unequivocally liveable and comfortable. There needed to be playful and imaginative zones for the children and calming wellness areas, as well as formal entertaining spaces. The clients, engaged from the beginning, wanted this home to reflect their vision of family life. Despite the architectural vastness of the space, it needed to be cocooning and personal, a family-oriented home. The project was a two and a half year journey, starting from a completely raw shell, beginning with full architectural and spatial planning and laying the foundations for how the home would function. This was followed by a base build fit out and bespoke interior architecture and tailored joinery, all overseen by the Elicyon Team. Finally, the curation of artwork and accessories with many pieces designed by Elicyon, as well as pieces commissioned by local artisans and craftspeople.

The interiors also needed to sit happily with the soaring birds-eye views. Dubai's soaring skyline became integral to the colour palette and the interior detail. “We felt the interiors had to stand up against the amazing views without fighting them,” says Gandhi. As such, the palette for this rare property on Palm Jumeirah was intentionally restrained, rich tactile fabrics evoking warmth and quiet opulence – including hand-painted Fromental silk wallpapers, bespoke leather detailing and finely woven textiles from Europe – were chosen. Soft cottons, velvets and tonal linens are used throughout the apartment, and the result is a home that does not compete with the sea and the sky but sits happily within it.

On the lower ground floor, the sculptural staircase in a bespoke liquid bronze finish curves elegantly to the floor above, architectural lighting of the marble treads creating a sense of excitement for what lies on the floor above. The double-height screens made from fabric mesh encased in glass separate the entrance and formal reception area. Here, in the formal reception, a crystal chandelier by Haberdashery spanning several metres in both height and width adds elegant fluidity to a room that is dominated by the view. Above the fireplace, a resin and bronze piece by Officina Coppola was commissioned especially for the space. The lower floor also features the family living room with generous sofas paired with circular onyx “Puddle” coffee tables, their soft curves a counterbalance to the room’s linear design. Further rooms on the lower floor include his and hers studies as well as a Moroccan hammam steam room with turquoise walls and domed niches inlaid with emerald mother of pearl and abalone shells.

On the upper floor, the main suite continues the colour palette of pale blues, greys and cream. Hand-painted silk wallpaper, Palissandro Bronze marble in cool blues with a subtle pink undertone for the tops of the bespoke bedside tables and a dressing area meticulously designed, tailored to the clients' requirements to display watches and jewellery, create a sense of easy elegance. For the children's bedrooms the team at Elicyon designed a floral motif developed in collaboration with Fahmeed Khalique; featuring bespoke, crafted leather flowers, one of the creations anoints the ceiling of the daughter's room.

“There is something poetic about Dubai apartments with soaring views,” says Gandhi, “we wanted to create a sanctuary where every detail carried a sense of storytelling linked to the location and this incredible bird’s eye perspective over the city and coastline.” But the real beauty of this apartment is that the interiors not only sit peacefully with the views of the vast skies and the sea, they also draw on elements that ground it, pulling this penthouse back to earth. The delicate birds perched on branches in the silk wallpaper, the oak leaf console by Cox London in the entrance lobby, the dining table a striking slab of tree trunk cleaved open by a resin river, all contribute to that feeling.
