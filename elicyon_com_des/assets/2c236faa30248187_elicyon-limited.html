About us

Founded in 2014 and based in their studio in Kensington Village, Elicyon is known for creating highly personalised, thoughtful and visionary interiors for its global clientele, with an unrivalled dedication to material, minutiae and modernity. Notable projects across the UK include private and listed homes in London's Belgrave Square, Regent's Park and St John's Wood, expansive apartments in the capital's leading super-prime developments, and family homes in the Lake District and on the Wentworth Estate in Surrey. On an international scale, Elicyon is working on several large-scale projects in New York, Miami and the Middle East. From interior architecture and design to project management and bespoke craftsmanship, the studio is primed to execute their client's vision with the utmost expertise, undeterred by complexity, scale or scene, from its creative conception through to the project’s completion.

    An insight from our Founder and Director, Charu Gandhi, on Elicyon’s collaboration with Lalique for WOW!house 2026. Following Lalique’s request for Elicyon to reimagine this year’s bar room, Charu reflects on the qualities that made the collaboration feel so aligned from the outset, from the brand’s extraordinary clarity of identity to its poetic approach to craftsmanship, materiality, and light. #Elicyon #Lalique #WOWhouse2026 #WOWhouseAtDCCH #DesignCollaboration #Craftsmanship #InteriorDesign

    Charu Gandhi
    3w

    When Lalique approached Elicyon to design the bar room for WOW!house 2026, what immediately resonated with me was the brand’s extraordinary clarity of identity. There is a poetic quality to the way Lalique works with material, light, and craftsmanship that immediately resonated with how we think about space at Elicyon. From the outset, the intention was to move beyond simply placing crystal within a room. Instead, we wanted Lalique’s crystal to become part of the architecture itself, integrated into how the space is experienced through reflection, texture, and atmosphere. What an amazing experience it has been with Lalique and the wonderful team Frederick Fischer, Jayne Boyd and Marc Larminaux. We look forward to unveiling The Lalique Bar by Elicyon at WOW!house 2026 from 2nd June - 2nd July at Design Centre, Chelsea Harbour. #Elicyon #Lalique #WOWhouse2026  #WOWhouseAtDCCH #InteriorDesign #DesignCollaboration #Craftsmanship #DesignLeadership

    Elicyon Trends 2026: Designing Outdoor Spaces. Outdoor spaces are increasingly shaped with the same intent as interiors, structured, layered and responsive to how they are used. Here’s a closer look at four defining approaches to outdoor design: Sculptural Planting: Planting is used with intent, introducing form, rhythm and structure to shape the landscape.⁠ The Art of Zoning: Outdoor areas can be structured into defined yet connected zones, creating space for entertaining, reflection, wellness and play. Each function is clearly expressed, while a consistent design language ensures continuity throughout. The Extended Interior: Tones and materials move fluidly from inside to out, creating a cohesive spatial language.⁠ Framing the View: Architecture and planting are composed to guide the eye, shaping outlooks and establishing a clear sense of direction. #Elicyon #DesignTrends2026 #OutdoorLiving #LandscapeDesign #InteriorArchitecture #DesignThinking #SpatialDesign #LondonDesign
